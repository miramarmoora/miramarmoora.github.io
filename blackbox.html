Claim

The amount of liquid poured into the box has to do with how much comes back out.

What We Used to Design

When making our personal and shared designs before the construction of our final model, we had to create an online prototype and to do that, we used Adobe Photoshop. When it did come time to build our final model and we completed the hands-on section of the unit, we built an online model of our final group model using Maya on Autodesk.

Argument

The Mystery Black Box is a box that we studied because of the unusual ways it works. When water was poured into the funnel, there was never a steady and consistent pattern in how much water came out. Sometimes more would come out, other times less would come out, and occasionally nothing would come out at all. We completed 50 different trials with 1000 mL poured into the box each time and took behind-the-scenes photos of how we ran the tests. Based on the data collected, I sketched my own personal design of what I thought was happening inside the box. After developing personal models, my group combined our ideas to create a final group model. Once the model was built, we tested it using two trials. In the first trial, we poured in 550 mL of water and got 300 mL out. In the second trial, we poured in 300.3 mL and got 100.4 mL out. We noticed that the output amount decreased with each trial. There were several limitations to our model, including differences in size between the real box and our mock-up, resource quality, and building time. The dependent variable was the amount of water coming out of the box, while the independent variable was the amount of water poured in. Our constants included using water in every trial. I stand by my claim that the amount of water poured into the box affects how much comes out. I believe there are two containers inside the box connected by tubes. One tube may be positioned higher than the other, causing different amounts of water to exit depending on how much water has already entered the system. I also think some water may be lost because the main tube does not always direct all the water into the containers, which could explain occasional leaks from the bottom of the box.

Self Reflection

I think I demonstrated communication and collaboration the best. I put myself out there more than I normally would because this was a hands-on activity. I shared my ideas, took risks, and learned from mistakes. Since I am usually very introverted, speaking up in my group was a big accomplishment for me. I suggested the water wheel idea and participated in the design process even though it was not included in the final model. I also made mistakes, such as not gluing a container high enough, but my group members helped me fix it. Overall, I am proud of how I worked with others throughout this project.
