    How is it done?

    Screen printing is a process used to put designs onto shirts, posters, and other materials. First, a screen made of fine mesh is prepared with a stencil that blocks out the areas where ink should not go. Next, the screen is placed on top of the item being printed. Ink is poured onto the screen, and a tool called a squeegee is used to push the ink through the open parts of the mesh. The ink passes through the screen and creates the design on the surface below. After the printing is finished, the ink is dried or heated so it stays on the material. This method is popular because it produces bright colors and can be used to make many copies of the same design.

    How did our quotes connect back to the real world?

    When we first began the screenprinting process, I found it boring. Nothing peaked my interest because it was all online design. I just chose a simple quote I thought was alluring and designed a simple photo with text to grab attention. Over time, I found myself becoming more attached to this photo and I couldn’t find out why. The more I looked at it, the more it just seemed like a normal photo of me in black and white with a quote. But then, it hit me. This photo had meaning, deeper than anyone could imagine. I found the quote tugging at my heart strings because it’s so true to real life. Success is never celebrated anymore. It’s better to keep secret then let anyone know you succeeded because envy is so real, and just as much as it is real, it’s painful. The hard work we all had to do really showed how hard it is to get such a simple and raw piece of advice out into the real world.
