Claim

The amount of liquid poured into the box has to do with how much comes back out.

What we used to design

When making our personal and shared designs before the construction of our final model, we had to create an online prototype and to do that, we used Adobe Photoshop. When it DID come time to build our final model and we completed the hands on section of the unit, we built an online model of our final group models using Maya on Autodesk.

Argument

The Mystery Black Box is a box that we studied into because of the weird ways it works. When water was poured into the funnel, there was never a steady and consistent pattern on how much water came out. Sometimes, more would come out, and other times less would come out, and occasionally, nothing at all would come out. The amount of liquid poured into the box has to do with how much comes back out. We did 50 different trials, 1000 mL being poured into the box each trial and we took behind the scene photos of how we ran the test exactly. Based on the data taken from the trials, I used that to sketch my own personal design on what I thought made the most sense and would work the best. After developing a personal model, me and my group built off of each other's models to create a final group model. Once it was finalized and built, we tested it by doing 2 trials and making the water input amount 550mL in the 1st trial and 300.3mL in the 2nd . What we noticed was that with each trial, the output amount decreased. Although we made a box built off of thoughts, there were several limitations such as the size difference between the real box and our mock up. As well as differences in resource quality, building time, etc. When creating a prototype of something, there are different components and different controls, as well as constants. For us, the dependent is the amount of water going in because we control it and the independent is the amount of water. Our controls are the amount of water being poured into the box and the amount of trials we choose to do. Our constant was the liquid being poured into the box which was water. If the amount of water going into the box didn’t have to do with how much comes out, then why else would the amount fluctuate depending on the amount of water that has been poured into the box already. For example, the more the number of trials went up, less water came out. We also observed that every trial, around 200mL less water came out then we poured in. Ex. input-500 output-300.2 (not our real data, just an example.) If the amount of water that went into the box did NOT have to do with how much came out, then the amount of water exiting would vary. I personally stand by my claim and I think that inside the black box, there are 2 containers and they both have tubes. One of the tubes comes out the bottom and on the other container, the tube is elevated higher on the container so that more and less water comes out depending on how much is poured. I think the water loss is from the main tube (the tube connecting to the funnel) missing the containers, which also explains why water (happens but it's rare) leaks out the bottom of the black box.
