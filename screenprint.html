    How is it done?

    Screen printing is a process used to put designs onto shirts, posters, and other materials. First, a screen made of fine mesh is prepared with a stencil that blocks out the areas where ink should not go. Next, the screen is placed on top of the item being printed. Ink is poured onto the screen, and a tool called a squeegee is used to push the ink through the open parts of the mesh. The ink passes through the screen and creates the design on the surface below. After the printing is finished, the ink is dried or heated so it stays on the material. This method is popular because it produces bright colors and can be used to make many copies of the same design.
