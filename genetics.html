    What Makes You, You?

    Genetics is the study of genes, heredity, and how traits are passed from parents to offspring. In this unit, we learned that DNA contains the genetic information that makes each person unique. To investigate DNA, we swabbed our cheek cells with toothpicks and used the PCR machine, which separates DNA based on their size.. During the process, DNA samples were placed into a gel and an electric current was applied, causing the DNA fragments to move through the gel. Smaller fragments traveled farther than larger fragments, creating a pattern of bands that could be analyzed. By examining these patterns, we were able to compare DNA samples and better understand how scientists study genetic information. This activity helped us see how DNA evidence can be used in genetics research, body identification, and forensic science.
